The Midnight Circle

Niamh Handley Vaughan

Producer & Co-Director of Midnight Circle

Niamh trained at Bath Spa University and attained a Performing Arts BA (Hons). Past theatre productions have included an open air touring production of Much Ado About Nothing, A Midsummer Night's Dream and Bunny by Jack Thorne. She has also performed in various musicals, short films and web-series, and been involved with productions at theatres such as The Arcola Theatre, The Tabbard and The Egg theatre Bath. Her first taste of immersive theatre was at the Bierkeller in Bristol in which she performed in part of a David Lynch dreamscape called Divine Nights.

Nadia Lamin

Producer & Co-Director of Midnight Circle

Nadia is an award winning actor for both Chimera (2017) & Human (2016) Short Films. After training at East 15 Acting School, her most recent theatre credits include: In the Shadow of Her Majesty (2023), Dorian Gray (2023) and Frankenstein (2023) playing the role of Creature which led to her nomination for Best Leading Role for Broadway World Awards. Her film credits: Feed Me (2023), Giddy Stratospheres (2021) both on Amazon Prime.

Miles Blanch

Associate Music Director, Performer

Miles is a producer, actor and musician from the Lake District who trained in acting at ALRA. Outside of the stage, Miles is one half of a roleplaying duo ‘Kobold & Co’ that run live immersive roleplaying shows as well as a weekly radio show/podcast and recently was in Lord Clegg Awakes – a Nick Clegg Musical.

Nicholas Benjamin

Associate Director, performer

Nicholas is an actor, writer, and director from North West London with over twenty years of experience. A University of Exeter graduate (2012), he began his career in theatre, performing in productions such as Sense and Sensibility, A Christmas Carol, A Midsummer Night’s Dream, Much Ado About Nothing and Dead Man Walking. His transition to film and TV brought critical acclaim, including Best Actor awards at the International White Deer Film Festival (2020) for Piggies and Film Oxford (2018) for Riff. His screen credits span major productions such as Batman (Warner Bros), The Crown (Netflix), Gangs of London (Sky Atlantic), Gangster Granny Strikes Again (BBC), and several documentaries for Discovery, History Channel, and Sky Crime. Alongside acting, Nicholas discovered a passion for immersive theatre directing, creating productions like Lost Words: The Tales of Edgar Allan Poe, The Glass Trilogy, and Dead Judgement. In 2019, he returned to directing with a planned immersive adaptation of Dracula, which, though cancelled due to the pandemic, rekindled his love for the craft.
